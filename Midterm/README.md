## Overview

This folder contains my midterm project for AI in Healthcare. For this assignment, I created **AI in Healthcare: A Glossary of Key Terms**, a research-based glossary of important concepts used in modern healthcare AI. The project was designed to strengthen my understanding of the language commonly used in clinical AI research, implementation, evaluation, and governance.

## Purpose of the Midterm

The purpose of this midterm was to build a strong foundation in healthcare AI terminology. In healthcare, understanding the vocabulary is essential because AI systems are discussed and evaluated by multidisciplinary teams that include clinicians, data scientists, informaticians, researchers, and healthcare administrators. This project helped me better understand how these terms are used in real clinical and operational settings.

## What This Project Includes

The midterm focuses on 30 key terms related to artificial intelligence in healthcare. Each term includes:

- A clear definition
- An example of how the term applies in healthcare
- A short explanation of why the term matters in real-world healthcare AI

The glossary covers a broad range of topics, including:

- Model development and evaluation
- Data quality and governance
- Privacy and de-identification
- Validation and generalizability
- Monitoring and post-deployment safety
- Human oversight and ethics
- Federated learning, foundation models, and transformers
- Real-world evidence and synthetic data

## Skills Demonstrated

Through this midterm project, I practiced and demonstrated the following skills:

- Research and source-based academic writing
- Technical and clinical vocabulary development
- Clear explanation of complex concepts
- Connecting AI terminology to healthcare examples
- Professional organization and documentation

## What I Learned

This project helped me understand that healthcare AI is not only about algorithms. It also depends on strong data governance, careful validation, privacy protection, ethical oversight, and continuous monitoring after deployment. I also learned that many important healthcare AI terms are closely connected. For example, concepts like calibration, external validation, generalizability, and model monitoring all play a major role in determining whether an AI tool is safe and useful in real clinical settings.

Another important takeaway from this assignment was that language matters. A shared understanding of these terms helps healthcare teams communicate more clearly and make better decisions about the design, evaluation, and responsible use of AI systems.
